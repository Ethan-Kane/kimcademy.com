Who I am

My name is Eric. I am a postgraduate at a top Canadian engineering university. I have been writing academically for over ten years, have attended a writing academy, won essay competitions, and consistently achieved the best grades in the history, psychology, English, and French courses I took throughout university in addition to the regular engineering curriculum. I've published academic journal papers, written thesis manuscripts, and eventually graduated with the highest academic average of my year.

 I have been teaching writing to students at the middle school all the way to the university level, and have even provided lessons on technical writing to other engineers. Lastly, having been through the French immersion program, along with an accredited Canadian bilingualism certification, I can also provide my programs and services for the French language.

Why I started Kimcademy

Since high school, I noticed that every one of my teachers adhered to a different set of writing rules. It seemed like everyone had different notions of what constituted a "proper" essay or paper! This was quite frustrating for a student like me, particularly because the feedback I would receive from my teachers with regards to my written work often contradicted one another. Some would tell me to never indent the first paragraph of an essay, others would disagree with the ways of using a comma.

 In any case, the confusion and uncertainty that arose from what is essentially a shortcoming of the education system was compounded by a significant lack of clear, consistent, and detailed instruction. Writing for school was like playing darts blindfolded. Sometimes, I would receive a high grade for a work I thought was mediocre, or othertimes, I would be dissapointed in my marks when I thought I had written a particularly thoughtful paper.

 Luckly, I had the opportunity to learn academic writing at a dedicated tutoring academy when I was still in high school. My writing quickly improved by a significant degree, to the point where the success I was having at school led me to enjoy writing. I continued to practice and improve my writing in university, where one of my history teachers actually told me I should have been an arts student after reading my final term paper.

 Eventually, after completing my undergraduate, which had involved writing a thesis manuscript, I figured others could benefit from the things I had learned along my academic journey. Whether you or your child wants to be an engineer, lawyer, or entrepreneur, learning to accurately convey thoughts through writing is a lifelong skill that becomes increasingly difficult to learn as people grow older and busier. If we can teach students to communicate well, they will undoubtedly have an advantage over the vast majority of individuals who have no such skill.

 Proficient writers are rarer than one might believe. Not everyone who graduates from high school are "good" writers. Not everyone who graduates from university even, are "good" writers. But proficient writing is less of a talent than a skill. It can be taught, and has been taught. I am proof of that and so are my students. So that is what I am here to do. Teach writing.
